Types of Fertilizers

Organic Fertilizers

Made from natural materials like compost, manure, and plant waste. They enrich the soil and improve its health over time.

Chemical Fertilizers

Contain minerals like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium that promote rapid plant growth and yield.

Bio Fertilizers

Contain living microorganisms like bacteria and fungi that help plants absorb nutrients naturally from the soil.

How to Make Fertilizers

Organic Fertilizer

Mix compost, dried leaves, and kitchen waste in a bin. Let it decompose for a few weeks until it turns into rich, dark soil. Use it to improve soil health naturally.

Banana Peel Fertilizer

Chop banana peels and soak them in water for 1‚Äì2 weeks. Strain and use this nutrient-rich water to feed your plants and boost growth.

Bio Fertilizer

Use beneficial bacteria or fungi available in local nurseries. Mix them into the soil to improve nutrient absorption naturally.

How to Use Fertilizers

Organic Fertilizer

Spread evenly around plant roots or mix into the topsoil. Water after applying to help nutrients penetrate the soil.

Chemical Fertilizer

Follow recommended dosage on the package. Apply carefully to soil, avoiding direct contact with leaves to prevent burning.

Bio Fertilizer

Mix into soil before planting or around established plants. Maintain soil moisture to allow microorganisms to thrive and enrich the soil.

Why Fertilizers Are Important?

Fertilizers provide essential nutrients that plants need for growth ‚Äî like nitrogen for leaves, phosphorus for roots, and potassium for fruits. Using the right type of fertilizer keeps the soil fertile and ensures sustainable agriculture for the future.
