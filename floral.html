🌸 India's Floral Heritage

Discover the blossoms that color India’s landscapes — from the sacred Lotus to the Himalayan Blue Poppy.

Lotus (Nelumbo nucifera)

India’s national flower, symbolizing purity, rebirth, and divine beauty — often linked to Goddess Lakshmi.

Marigold (Tagetes erecta)

Bright orange and yellow blooms widely used in festivals and weddings, representing positivity and energy.

Jasmine (Jasminum sambac)

Known as “Mogra,” its fragrance fills Indian nights. A symbol of love and purity, often used in garlands.

Rose (Rosa indica)

The queen of flowers in India — representing love, devotion, and the timeless beauty of nature.

Rhododendron (Buransh)

Found in the Himalayas, its bright red flowers are used to make juices and have medicinal properties.

Hibiscus (Gudhal)

Sacred to Goddess Kali, this red flower is also valued for hair and skin treatments in Ayurveda.
